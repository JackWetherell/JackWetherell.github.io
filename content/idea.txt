        <div class="contentTitle"><h1><a href="http://www-users.york.ac.uk/~rwg3/idea.html">The interacting Dynamic Electrons Approach (iDEA) code</a></h1></div>
        <div class="contentText">

        <img src="files/idea_logo.png" alt="The iDEA code" style="border:2px solid white;float:right;">
	The goal of the iDEA code is to improve the accuracy of approximations within fundamental theories of many-electron quantum mechanics. The 		iDEA code is a Python software suite developed in Rex Godby's group at the University of York since 2010 in 		collaboration with others. It has a central role in a number of research projects related to many-electron quantum mechanics for electrons 		in matter. <br><br>

	<b>iDEA's main features:</b>
	<ul>
 	<li>Exact solution of the many-electron (time-dependent) Schr√∂dinger equation, including exact exchange and correlation</li>
  	<li>Simplicity and exact solutions which approach the degree of exchange and correlation in realistic systems achieved using spinless  		electrons in one dimension</li>
  	<li>An arbitrary external potential that may be time-dependent</li>
 	<li>Optimisation methods to determine the exact (time-dependent) Kohn-Sham potential and energy components of (time-dependent) density 		functional theory</li>
  	<li>Implementation of various approximate functionals (established and novel) for comparison</li>
  	<li>Established and novel localisation measures</li>
  	<li>Many-body perturbation theory</li>
	</ul> 
        <br>
	
	<b>The next step:</b>
	Once more reliable approximations are devised, the next crucial step is for these new methods to be implemented into large-scale codes 		which model realistic systems in order to establish their accuracy. If these new methods prove to be improvements on existing methods, they 		can then be used to model a wider range of physical systems enhancing our knowledge of materials and chemicals. <br><br>
